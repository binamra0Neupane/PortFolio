Research

NP-complete Problem

As an Honors student in my Data Structures and Algorithms course, I was given the opportunity to conduct independent research on computational complexity. I chose to explore NP-Complete problems and focused on a custom variant called the Magnets Problem, which involves determining whether a given set of symbols (like lettered magnets) can be fully used to form valid words from a predefined vocabulary. I formally proved that the problem is NP-Complete via a reduction from the 3-Dimensional Matching problem. This work was documented in an IEEE-formatted research paper, which includes a detailed proof, background on NP-Completeness, and a discussion on adapting approximation algorithms from the Vertex Cover problem to similar challenges.
